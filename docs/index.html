The Pattern

Early Movers (Pre-2020)

Peter Thiel and contrarian figures who never aligned with progressive tech culture. The ideological vanguard.

COVID Era Converts (2020-2022)

Musk, Andreessen, Sacks—radicalized by pandemic restrictions and progressive employee activism.

2024 Pragmatists

Zuckerberg, Bezos, Pichai—regulatory pressure and business calculation drove late-stage alignment.

The Tax Catalyst

Harris's proposed 25% unrealized gains tax on $100M+ fortunes terrified founders. Musk, Bezos, Zuckerberg faced tens of billions in one-time bills. Andreessen's "Little Tech Agenda" called it "the death of startups."

Inauguration Conformists (2025)

Cook and others who simply followed power, attending Trump's inauguration regardless of prior positions.

The Harris Tax Factor

Kamala Harris's endorsement of a 25% unrealized capital gains tax on fortunes over $100 million became a rallying cry for tech opposition. Unlike traditional capital gains taxes triggered by selling assets, this would tax paper wealth annually—forcing founders to sell shares or take loans just to pay the IRS.

Estimated One-Time Tax Bills

  • Elon Musk: $50-100+ billion
  • Mark Zuckerberg: $30-40 billion
  • Jeff Bezos: $30-40 billion

Andreessen Horowitz's "Little Tech Agenda" declared the proposal would "absolutely kill both startups and the venture capital industry." This framing—that Democratic tax policy threatened the existence of Silicon Valley—gave ideological cover for Trump endorsements.

Sources: Fortune · a16z Little Tech Agenda · Tax Foundation

Methodology

"Switch date" is defined as the first public action demonstrating clear Republican/Trump alignment: endorsement, major donation, policy reversal, or attendance at key Trump events. Vertical position reflects the chronological order of switching, with earliest at top.
