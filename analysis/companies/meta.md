# Meta (Facebook) - The Most Dramatic Reversal

## Company Overview
- Founded: 2004 (as Facebook)
- Rebranded: 2021 (to Meta)
- CEO: Mark Zuckerberg (founder, controlling shareholder)
- Employees: ~67,000 (after layoffs)
- Market Cap: ~$1.5T
- Products: Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Threads, Quest VR

## The DEI Arc

### 2014-2019: DEI Build-Up

#### Public Commitments
- 2014: First diversity report published
- Annual diversity reports with specific metrics
- Public hiring goals by race/gender
- "Managing Unconscious Bias" training company-wide
- Chief Diversity Officer position created (Maxine Williams, 2013)
- Partnerships with HBCUs and diversity organizations

#### Hiring Initiatives
- Diverse slate requirements
- Rooney Rule adoption
- Pipeline programs for underrepresented groups
- University recruiting diversity focus
- Employee Resource Groups (ERGs) funded and promoted

#### Internal Culture
- Employee activism encouraged
- Internal social groups for advocacy
- "Bring your whole self to work" messaging
- Social justice statements on company channels

### 2020: Peak DEI

#### George Floyd Response
- Zuckerberg Facebook post supporting BLM
- $10M donation to racial justice organizations
- Internal town halls on racism
- Employee walkout over Trump posts (Zuckerberg allowed them)
- Commitment to increase Black representation

#### COVID-Era Content Moderation
- Aggressive misinformation labeling
- "Authoritative sources" promotion
- Trump posts labeled/restricted
- Health misinformation policies strict

#### 2020 Election
- $400M donation to election administration (CTCL, CEIR)
- "Zuckerbucks" became right-wing epithet
- Election integrity initiatives
- Trump eventually banned (Jan 2021)

### 2021-2023: Quiet Retreat

#### Financial Pressure
- 2022: 11,000 layoffs (first major layoffs ever)
- 2023: 10,000 more layoffs
- DEI team among those cut
- "Year of Efficiency" declared

#### Shifting Priorities
- Metaverse focus (distraction from DEI)
- Less public diversity reporting
- Hiring goals quietly dropped
- Internal activism increasingly discouraged

#### Trump Return
- Trump reinstated on Facebook (2023)
- Content moderation loosening begins
- Less aggressive fact-checking

### 2024-2025: Full Reversal

#### Policy Changes
- **Fact-checking ended** (replaced with "Community Notes")
- **DEI programs cut** (confirmed January 2025)
- **Diversity team disbanded**
- **Hiring goals eliminated**
- Supplier diversity programs ended
- Training programs removed

#### Leadership Changes
- Maxine Williams (Chief Diversity Officer) - role diminished
- Dana White (UFC CEO, Trump ally) joins board
- Joel Kaplan (Republican) elevated to Chief Global Affairs Officer
- Nick Clegg departed

#### Cultural Shift
- "Masculine energy" comments from Zuckerberg
- Joe Rogan podcast appearance (cultural signal)
- Less San Francisco, more "founder mode"
- Internal activism suppressed

#### Political Alignment
- Trump congratulation call
- Mar-a-Lago dinner
- $1M inaugural donation
- Zuckerberg at inauguration prominently seated

## Employee Exodus/Transformation

### Who Left
- Trust & Safety employees
- Content moderation teams (outsourced)
- DEI professionals
- Progressive engineers (self-selected out)
- Communications/policy staff

### Who Remained/Joined
- Engineering focus
- "Mission-aligned" employees
- Less activist workforce
- Dana White (board)
- Trump-friendly executives

## The Business Case Analysis

### For DEI (2014-2020)
- Advertiser demands
- Talent acquisition (competing with Google)
- Public pressure
- Regulatory defense (appear responsible)
- User growth in diverse markets

### Against DEI (2024-2025)
- Conservative advertiser/user threat
- Regulatory relief from Trump
- Employee cost reduction
- "Efficiency" framing
- X competition for conservative users
