# Marc Andreessen - The Ideological Warrior

## Background
- Born: 1971, Iowa
- Created: Mosaic browser, Netscape
- Firm: Andreessen Horowitz (a16z) - co-founded 2009
- Net Worth: ~$2B+
- Role: Tech ideologist, kingmaker VC

## Political Timeline

### 1990s-2000s: Tech Optimist
- Netscape founder - internet pioneer
- Generally liberal Silicon Valley figure
- Believed in tech as democratizing force
- Supported Obama

### 2010s: VC Power Broker
- a16z became dominant tech VC
- Invested in Facebook, Twitter, Airbnb, Coinbase, etc.
- Growing frustration with San Francisco politics
- Housing NIMBY battles radicalized him

### 2020-2022: Radicalization Accelerates
- COVID restrictions infuriated him
- "It's time to build" essay (April 2020)
- Increasing anti-government rhetoric
- Clubhouse political discussions revealed views
- Left mainstream corporate boards (Meta board exit)

### 2023: Public Political Turn
- "Techno-Optimist Manifesto" (October 2023)
- Explicitly anti-"woke," anti-DEI
- Praised controversial figures
- Identified "enemies" including social responsibility

### 2024: All-In on Trump
- **March**: Published "Little Tech Agenda"
- **July**: Endorsed Trump publicly with Ben Horowitz
- Podcast appearances (Rogan, All-In, etc.)
- Major Trump campaign fundraising
- Crypto policy primary motivation stated

### 2025: Victory Lap
- Prominent at Trump inauguration
- Direct White House access
- Crypto/AI policy influence
- Vindication narrative

## "Techno-Optimist Manifesto" - Key Points

### The Ideology
- Technology is inherently good
- Markets solve all problems
- Regulation is the enemy
- "Effective accelerationism" (e/acc) adjacent
- Population growth good
- Energy abundance required
- AI will save humanity

### The Enemies (his word)
- ESG
- DEI
- "Sustainability"
- "Social responsibility"
- "Tech ethics"
- Risk management
- Trust and safety

### Praised Figures
- Ayn Rand
- Milton Friedman
- Various controversial tech figures

## "Little Tech Agenda" - Policy Platform

### Published March 2024
Co-authored with Ben Horowitz as explicit a16z political manifesto

### Key Demands
1. No AI regulation
2. Crypto regulatory clarity (pro-industry)
3. Startup-friendly tax policy
4. Reduced antitrust enforcement
5. Immigration reform (H-1B expansion)
6. Anti-"de-banking" (crypto companies)

### Framing
- "Little tech" vs "big tech"
- Startups oppressed by regulation
- Democrats hostile to innovation
- Only Republicans will help
